## Universal recruiter rules

1. **Don't be too eager.** Confidence > desperation.
2. **Always ask comp.** Recruiters who refuse = waste of time.
3. **Reply within 48 hrs.** They move fast.
4. **Don't ghost.** Even "no thanks" is professional.
5. **Track every recruiter** in `tracker/interviews/job-tracker.md` — they remember.
6. **Recruiters are not your friend.** They optimize for placement, not your career. Use them for leads, not strategy.

## Recruiter quality signals

✅ **Good recruiter:**
- In-house at target company (not agency)
- Knows the tech stack details
- Shares comp band proactively
- Specific about role responsibilities
- Reasonable timeline (2-4 weeks to hire)

🚩 **Red flags:**
- "Great opportunity, can't share details until call" (gatekeeper)
- "Comp depends on your experience" (opaque)
- Generic mass-DM ("I have several roles...")
- Pushes hard for call w/o info
- Agency w/ unclear company name

## Comp negotiation in first reply

If they share a band ($120-150K), reply: "That band would work for the right role + team. Open to hearing more."

If they share a number ($120K): "Above $140K + good equity is what I'm targeting given my recent AI work and offers in flight" (mention competition even if not yet true — common practice).

If they ghost on comp: move on.
