# Warm Intro Request (friend-of-friend)

**Channel:** Same as their preferred channel
**Goal:** Get introduced to a 2nd-degree contact
**Target reply rate:** 90%+

---

## Template (asking your friend to intro you)

Hi [Friend Name],

Quick ask — I noticed you're connected with [Target Name] at [Company]. I'm in the middle of an AI Engineer job hunt and [Company] is high on my list. Would you be open to making an intro?

I'll write the intro email for you so it's a 30-second forward. No pressure if you don't know them well or it's awkward.

Here's a draft you can use:

---

**Subject:** Intro — [Your Name] (AI Engineer) ↔ [Target Name]

Hi [Target Name],

Wanted to introduce [Your Name] — a sharp AI Engineer who shipped 5 production fintech AI projects in 6 months ([github.com/yourhandle]). They're exploring [Company] and I thought you two might enjoy a chat — even if it's just shooting the breeze about AI in fintech.

[Your Name], over to you to take it from here.

[Friend's signature]

---

Let me know if you're game and I'll send anything else helpful (resume, project links).

Thanks!
[Your name]

---

## Variations

### When friend doesn't know target well
"Even a 'hey I don't know X super well but Y wanted to say hi' is enough — no pressure to vouch."

### When you want to soften the ask
"Totally fine to say no — I know intros are social capital. Just thought I'd ask."

### When friend is at the target company themselves
Use the [referral-ask.md](referral-ask.md) template instead.

---

## After they intro you

1. Reply to BOTH (friend + target) within 4 hrs
2. Move convo to private channel w/ target (don't keep friend on every email)
3. Thank friend separately ("intro went well, talking next week")
4. Always update friend on outcome — they invested social capital

## What NOT to do

- ❌ Ask friend to write the intro from scratch (do the work for them)
- ❌ Ask friend to vouch for skills they don't know
- ❌ Forget to thank publicly (LinkedIn comment, X mention) when it works out
- ❌ Skip updates — they want to know if their effort paid off
- ❌ Use this for cold targets (4+ degrees away — won't work)

## When this works best

- Friend is also in tech (understands the value)
- Target has known you for years (warm trust transfers)
- You're asking for a SHORT intro (not a 1-hour informational interview)
- You provide the draft (frictionless)
